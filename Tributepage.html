Ada Lovelace was an English mathematician and writer,chiefly known for her work on Charles's Babbage's proposed mechanical general-purpose computer,the Analytical Engine. She was the first to recognise that the machine had applications beyond pure calculation, and created the first algorithm intended to be carried out by such a machine. As a result,she is often regarded as the first to recognise the full potential of a "computing machine" and the first computer programmer.
1815

Born in London, England. She was the only legitimate child of the poet Lord Byron and his wife Anna Isabella "Annabella" Milbanke.
1842

Translated the Italian mathematician Luigi Menabrea's article on the Analytical Engine.In her notes, she describes an algorithm for the Analytical Engine to compute Bernoulli numbers.
1844

Desired to create a mathematical model for the brain creates thoughts and visited the electrical Engineer Andrew Crosse learn how to carry out electrical experiments.
1852

Died from uterine cancer. She was buried, at her request, next to her father at the Church of St.Mary Magdalene in Hucknail, Nottinghamshire.
